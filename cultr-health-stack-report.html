03 / CRM Layer

Layer 1 — Patient Acquisition

HubSpot — what it does & doesn't do

✅ What HubSpot handles

✓ Lead capture from quiz, waitlist, landing pages
✓ Automated email sequences (nurture → convert)
✓ Sales pipeline: lead → consultation → member
✓ Creator/affiliate contact tracking
✓ Churn prevention & re-engagement campaigns
✓ Signs BAA on paid plans (HIPAA-safe for contact data)

🚫 What HubSpot does NOT do

✗ Clinical charting or provider notes
✗ E-prescribing or Rx management
✗ Lab result storage (don't put PHI here)
✗ Replace your EMR in any way
⚠️ Key rule: Keep clinical data out of HubSpot. Contact info (name, email, source) only. PHI lives in the EMR.
Free tier available Starter ~$20/mo BAA required on paid plan

05 / E-Prescribing

E-Prescribing Infrastructure

DoseSpot — and when you need it

What is E-Prescribing?

→ Sends prescriptions electronically to pharmacies
→ Routes through Surescripts — the national Rx network
→ Required for all controlled substances (EPCS)
→ NPs must be credentialed in each state they prescribe

DoseSpot Specifically

✓ Surescripts-certified, API-first
✓ Embeds into custom apps via iframe/API
✓ Most used by telehealth startups
✓ EPCS support (controlled substances)
✓ Decision: Akute Health includes e-prescribing on all plans. Add DoseSpot only if Akute's EPCS coverage is insufficient for the controlled substance compounds your NPs prescribe. Confirm EPCS on your Akute sales call.
⚠️ Verify: Schedule III–V coverage in FL DoseSpot ~$100–200/mo if needed
06 / CRM Answer

The Direct Answer

Does this stack solve our CRM problem?

✅ Yes — top of funnel is fully covered. HubSpot handles lead capture, email nurture, quiz follow-ups, waitlist, creator contacts, and re-engagement. This is what most people mean by "CRM."
⚠️ Partially — the lead-to-patient handoff is a gap. When a prospect becomes a paying member, you manually add them to Akute. There's no auto-sync between HubSpot and your EMR — and that's intentional (PHI must not flow into HubSpot).
📋 Missing piece: Post-sale retention lives in two places — HubSpot (email/marketing) and Akute (clinical). You need a clear internal process for which tool owns which touchpoint.

Ownership map — who touches the patient at each stage

Quiz/Lead → HubSpot → Checkout → Your Next.js app → Intake → Akute EMR → Labs → SiPhox portal → Retention → HubSpot
07 / SiPhox

Lab Data Strategy

SiPhox Health — portal vs. dashboard

Option A — SiPhox Native Portal

✓ Zero dev effort
✓ Always up to date
✗ Patients leave CULTR experience
✗ No unified view with orders/billing
✗ Admin can't see order status in-app

Option B — Pull via API (Recommended)

✓ Labs inside CULTR dashboard (branded)
✓ Admin sees order status + results
✓ Unified with bills, Rx history
⚠ Requires patient OAuth link to SiPhox
⚠ Confirm SiPhox Connect API supports token delegation
✓ Recommended: Use Option B. Patient self-directs the SiPhox kit. On first login to CULTR portal, patient links their SiPhox account (OAuth). Your Next.js app pulls results via connect.siphoxhealth.com/api and displays them alongside orders and billing.
⚠️ Confirm: SiPhox Connect API supports read access for partners
